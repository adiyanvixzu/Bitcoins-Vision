Bitcoins Vision

On October 31, 2008, Satoshi Nakamoto published a whitepaper on a new electronic cash system that's fully peer-to-peer, with no trusted third party, called Bitcoin. Bitcoin and blockchain technology are so advanced and forward thinking, that it is literally changing the world before our eyes.

What is the vision for Bitcoin? This video below does a good job of explaining that the technology behind crypto currencies such as Bitcoin will inevitably and radically change the role of traditional trusted parties such as banks, accountants, notaries, and governments. The possibilities are endless. We invite you to explore this new technology constructively and critically, and have an open discussion about the potential applications.



Where do I begin?


In order to obtain bitcoins, you need to first purchase them. Bitcoins are divisible, just like dollars. You don't need to buy a whole bitcoin to get started. You can buy fractions of a bitcoin. You can buy bitcoins from places like Coinbase, Circle, or Cubits. It might depend on where you are located too, on where you purchase your bitcoins. BitcoinX provides an updated list of bitcoin exchanges to buy from, including a list by country location. If you don't want to buy bitcoins from an online exchange, you can purchase them in person using an "over-the-counter" platform such as LocalBitcoins.

How do I keep my bitcoins safe?


Securing your bitcoins is paramount. Just like cash, you want to keep your money safe. This means moving it off of a bitcoin exchange after purchasing them, and storing them in a bitcoin wallet. There are many different kinds of wallets such as Blockchain, Armory, or Electrum. The choice is yours on where you store your bitcoins; we suggest that you try out a couple before making a final decision on what works best for you. Instead of using a wallet, you have the option of keeping your bitcoins in cold storage.

Are you a merchant?


If so, you can accept bitcoins from your customers for payment. Merchants can begin receiving the benefits of bitcoin's low transaction fees and fast transaction times in just a few steps. It's easy, using existing merchant platforms like BitPay or Coinbase for online purchases, or the Blockchain app for in-store POS transactions. If you're looking for a physical bitcoin machine for your store, Coinkite has exchange terminals.

Where can I spend bitcoins?


Spending bitcoins has become much easier over the years as bitcoin is being more widely adopted and services are being created to make using bitcoins simple. An easy place to search for places to spend bitcoins is Spendabit. If you're looking for a brick and mortar store, check out Coinmap. Or, use Gyft to purchase a gift card with bitcoins!

Where can I follow bitcoin news?


There are a few bitcoin news sites, but these are highly recommended: Coinfire, CoinTelegraph, NewsBTC, Bitcoin Magazine, and CoinDesk. Alternatively, you can monitor bitcoin news on Twitter, for quick updates using this Bitcoin List.

I'm a developer, how can I help bitcoin?


The bitcoin developer community is growing, and is always looking for devs to help build new and exciting ways to use bitcoin and the blockchain. You can help by contributing to the bitcoin core protocol. Ask technical questions on Stack Exchange, or look up technical information on the Bitcoin Wiki. You can also subscribe to the bitcoin dev email discussion list. Or, just build your own bitcoin related tool, application, or service.
